About



Hi there! I am Ferran Martinez i Coma.


I am an applied political scientist living in Brisbane and working at Griffith University.
Here you can find my project Global Voter Turnout, a database covering
all national elections turnout rates from 1945 to 2020.

Introduction

This website presents the Global Voter Turnout Database, including the Codebook and related publications.

Publications

The publications below have sparked my interest on creating
the Global Voter Turnout Database.

  • 2021 - Richard W. Frank and Ferran Martinez i Coma. “Correlates of Voter Turnout” Political Behavior, https://doi.org/10.1007/s11109-021-09720-y

  • 2021 - Ignacio Lago and F. Martinez i Coma. “Transaction costs in electoral coordination: How turnout shapes changes in the number of parties” Electoral Studies, 72.  https://doi.org/10.1016/j.electstud.2021.102349

  • 2020 - F. Martinez i Coma and Lee Morgenbesser. “Election turnout in authoritarian regimes”, Electoral Studies, 68. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.electstud.2020.102222

  • 2018 - F. Martinez i Coma and Annika Werner. “Compulsory voting and ethnic diversity increase invalid voting while corruption does not: an analysis of 417 parliamentary elections in 73 countries” Democratization, 26(2): 288-308 https://doi.org/10.1080/13510347.2018.1524879

  • 2017 - F. Martinez i Coma and A. Nai. “Ethnic Diversity Decreases Turnout. Comparative Evidence from 650 Elections around the World”, Electoral Studies 49: 75-95 https://doi.org/10.1016/j.electstud.2017.07.002

  • 2017 - R. W. Frank and F. Martinez i Coma “How election dynamics shape perceptions of electoral integrity”  Electoral Studies 48: 153-165 https://doi.org/10.1016/j.electstud.2017.05.007

  • 2017 - F. Martinez i Coma and M. Trinh, “ How electoral integrity affects voter turnout in democracies”, Australian Journal of Political Science 52(1): 53-74. http://dx.doi.org/10.1080/10361146.2016.1238869

  • 2016 - F. Martinez i Coma, “Turnout Determinants in Democracies and in Non-Democracies,” Electoral Studies Volume 41:50-59 https://doi.org/10.1016/j.electstud.2015.11.002
